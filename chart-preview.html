Section 01
Key Insights
4 insights identified
1
Users don't understand the angled mounting plate
Confusion about what it is and when they need it
2
Asking users to decide before they understand
Decision presented before context is established
↗
Shift: Asking → Guiding
Core reframe of the entire flow
4
Don't ask technical decisions users don't understand
Remove friction by removing premature choices
⚡
Section 02
Action Plan
5 actions · 1 highest priority
1
Redesign decision logic
Replace "Do you need angled plate?" with situation-based guidance
Highest Priority
2
Combine steps
Merge "need plate?" + "left/right" into "What does your setup look like?"
3
Improve clarity of visuals
Clearer POV — show what the camera sees
4
Add education at the right moment
Contextual, not an upfront dump
5
Validate with real data
Customer Care data + Nightingale reference
🎨
Section 03
Design Options
3 ranked options
🥇
Option A — Scenario-based
Recommended
Show doorbell position → if angled, introduce plate → choose direction. Most natural, least cognitive load.
🥈
Option B — Guided questions
"Is your doorbell facing the entryway directly?" → Yes / No branching flow. Clear but slightly more steps.
🥉
Option C — Educational first
Explain the plate, then ask if needed. Less ideal — front-loads complexity before establishing need.
